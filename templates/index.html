    What is "Roster Format"?

    In order for the pay checker to accurately process a roster file selected by the user, it has to know some information ahead of time. One very useful piece of information is what 'format' a roster is in. This refers to the orientation of the rows and columns in the roster - specifically whether the dates are in rows or columns, where the shift times are located, and where the employee's name is located. There are three possible roster formats:

    Type A

    Dates horizontally, shift times vertically, and names in the spreadsheet body.

    Type B

    Dates horizontally, names vertically, and shift times in the spreadsheet body.

    Type C

    Names horizontally, dates vertically, and shift times in the spreadsheet body.
